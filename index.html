My First Michigan State Football Game

Published: September 15, 2025
Author: Eli Klunder

As a freshman at Michigan State University, I was excited for many things, but nothing compared to the anticipation of my first football game at Spartan Stadium. The energy on campus was electric all week, with students wearing green and white, and the marching band practicing in the distance. On game day, I met up with my new friends, all of us decked out in MSU gear, faces painted and ready to cheer.

Walking to the stadium, I was amazed by the sea of fans tailgating, grilling, and playing cornhole. The smell of burgers and the sound of laughter filled the air. When we finally entered the stadium, the roar of the crowd was overwhelming. The band played the fight song, and everyone sang along. I felt a sense of belonging and pride that I had never experienced before.

The game itself was thrilling. Every touchdown was met with deafening cheers, and the student section was wild with excitement. I learned the chants, high-fived strangers, and even got a free t-shirt tossed into the crowd. By the end of the night, my voice was hoarse, but my heart was full. Walking the Stage: Graduation Day Reflections

Published: May 5, 2025
Author: Eli Klunder

Graduation day at Michigan State University was a moment I had dreamed about for years. The journey to this point was filled with late-night study sessions, group projects, and countless memories with friends. As I put on my cap and gown, I felt a mix of excitement, pride, and a little bit of nervousness. The campus looked beautiful, with spring flowers in bloom and families gathering to celebrate.

The ceremony was held in the Breslin Center, packed with graduates and their loved ones. As my name was called and I walked across the stage, I was overwhelmed by a sense of accomplishment. All the hard work, the challenges, and the growth over the past four years had led to this moment. I shook hands with the dean, received my diploma, and smiled for the camera, knowing my family was cheering in the crowd.

After the ceremony, I joined my friends for photos around campus. We reminisced about our favorite classes, professors, and the adventures we had together. Graduation was not just an ending, but a new beginning. As I looked ahead to the future, I felt ready to take on whatever came next, grateful for the experiences and friendships that MSU had given me.
